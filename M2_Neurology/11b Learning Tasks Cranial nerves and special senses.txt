Neurology XIII:  Cranial nerves and special senses

Learning tasks:

Define vertigo, and define the features to distinguish peripheral from central vertigo and benign paroxysmal positional vertigo from central vertigo.
Describe the clinical assessment of visual function.
Describe amaurosis fugax, anterior ischemic optic neuropathy and optic neuritis.
Describe the anatomy and clinical symptoms and localization for diplopia.
Describe the pathophysiology of hearing and distinguish conductive from sensorineural hearing loss
Describe trigeminal neuralgia.
Distinguish central facial weakness from Bell’s palsy
Describe blepherospasm
Describe bulbar and pseudobulbar palsy
Describe how the cranial nerves can be involved in cavernous sinus syndromes and pontocerebellar angle tumors
